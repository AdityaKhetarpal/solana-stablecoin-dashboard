TL;DR
Solana hit $16.8B in supply, processed $1.8T in Q1 transfers, and held firm as SOL crashed 41% - it is now running as global payment infrastructure.

01 - Supply

$16.8B ATH in February

Stablecoin supply on Solana peaked at $16.8B on Feb 16 - a 3× increase since end-2024. The ecosystem surpassed BSC to become the 3rd-largest stablecoin chain globally.

02 - Composition

USDC holds 55%, but USDG is rising

USDC maintained its dominant position at ~55% of supply ($8.6B). USDG (Global Dollar) emerged as a top-3 Solana stablecoin, growing to $1.1B+ by quarter-end - outpacing PYUSD.

03 - Resilience

Supply decoupled from SOL price

SOL fell 41% from its Jan peak of ~$145. Stablecoin supply dropped only 6.5% from ATH - holding above $15.7B through March. Stablecoins are now independent from SOL's volatility cycle.

Regulatory Tailwinds
GENIUS Act & MiCA Accelerate Adoption
Key regulatory developments Q1 2026
i
Regulatory analysis from Cointelegraph reporting on the GENIUS Act (US) and EU MiCA framework implementation in Q1 2026.
GENIUS ACT (US)
1:1 reserve mandate - USDC's structural advantage
The GENIUS Act mandates 1:1 cash/Treasury reserves per stablecoin issued with no idle yield. Circle (USDC) was already structured this way - boosting institutional trust and driving USDC's 7.42% Q1 global supply growth to $78B.
MiCA (EU)
Euro stablecoin supply hit $1.1B (+3x YoY)
EU MiCA framework issued e-money licenses enabling euro-denominated stablecoins (EURCV) to launch on Solana and Stellar. Non-USD stablecoin volumes reached $10B in Q1 (+1,600% YoY), driven by cross-border remittances and B2B payments.
Key takeaway
Regulatory clarity in both the US and EU is compressing the institutional risk premium on stablecoins. Solana's compliance-friendly architecture positions it as the preferred chain for regulated payment flows heading into Q2.
